How to run the nbn throughput test
The nbn app (available on iOS and Android) can run a throughput test directly from your NTD — the white nbn box on your wall. This test bypasses your router and WiFi, measuring the speed delivered to your premises rather than to your device. This makes it the most reliable way to tell whether a performance issue is within the nbn network or within your home.

Open the nbn app → My Services → Run a speed test. Record the download and upload results below.

Mathis Results

Mathis formula: Throughput = (MSS / RTT) × (1 / √p) — upper bound for a single TCP flow under steady-state AIMD congestion control. Thresholds: <70% PIR = Below threshold · 70–80% = Marginal · ≥80% = Acceptable.

Layer 2 vs speed test — why the numbers differ
The PIR is quoted at Layer 2 (Ethernet frame rate on the PON). The Mathis formula estimates throughput at the TCP layer — already ~3–5% below PIR due to IP and TCP header overhead. Ookla and the nbn app measure application-layer goodput, which sits above TCP, so they read lower again due to connection ramp-up. A customer recording 93–97% of PIR on Ookla is typically performing at or near the service ceiling. The full stack: PIR (L2) > Mathis (L4) > Speed test (L7).
